Miquel Sendra is a developmental biologist interested in how cells acquire their fate. He studied biotechnology and gradually moved into data analysis during his PhD, where he combined experiments with computational approaches, focusing on image analysis and genomic data.

Now, as a postdoctoral researcher in Leo Guignard’s lab, he is further specializing in computational methods, using programming and quantitative analysis to study Parhyale hawaiensis. He enjoys working in a multidisciplinary environment—and when he’s not in the lab, you can probably find him rock climbing in Les Calanques.
